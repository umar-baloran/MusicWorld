Music Review : Happy New Year
Director :  Farah Khan
Music :  Vishal-Shekhar, Manj Musik and Dr. Zeus
Lyrics :  Irshad Kamil, Kumaar, Vishal-Shekhar, Jiwan Mann and Farah Khan
Starring :  Shah Rukh Khan, Deepika Padukone, Abhishek Bachchan, Sonu Sood, Boman Irani, Vivaan Shah and Jackie Shroff

September 19, 2014 2:52:30 PM IST

updated September 19, 2014 5:54:26 PM IST

By Rafat, Glamsham Editorial

The last two times that director Farah Khan and the Badshah of Bollywood Shah Rukh Khan teamed up for MAIN HOON NA and OM SHANTI OM, they redefined the Hindi film music scene with the musical offerings. Even Farah's last venture, TEES MAAR KHAN (featuring Akshay Kumar), which failed commercially, boasted of popular pathbreaking music ('Sheila Ki Jawani' showed the way for graceful, cute yet sexy item number without being raunchy or crude). So with gigantic expectations one checks out the music of their Diwali extravaganza HAPPY NEW YEAR which promises to be loads of fun, entertaining and crackling.

The song which has already become an anthem of sorts for all us Indians, 'Indiawaale' is so very catchy, so very addictive that one just gets hooked on to the song in just the first hear. The rhythm is catchy, the musical orchestrations just perfect and the renditions of all the singers, Vishal Dadlani, KK, Shankar Mahadevan and Neeti Mohan just superb. Irshad Kamil's magical lyrics are so very appealing and identifiable that any and every Indian (and all India lovers from different countries!) will surely patronize the song in a big way.

The electronic version of 'Indiawaale' is well done and is simply not a mere space filler and makes for fun hear with lots of electronic guiatring and addendums.

The all romantic track, 'Manwa Laage', is another song that one instantly falls in love with instantly. And the reasons are a plenty! Arijit Singh and Shreya Ghoshal, the prince and queen of romantic numbers together are just excellent, lyrics (Irshad Kamil) which are in simple 'desi' Hindi and the lovely melody. Simply chartbusting!

The retro feel (tune wise) is apparent in 'Satakli', a nice situational number that is devotional and festive too in nature. Sukhwinder Singh's rendition is perfect as always. The all out Hindustani musical instrumentations complete with 'band baaja' and Irshad's lyrics, that compare life to a girl who cannot be courted or wooed, makes for a fun hear.

Dr.Zeus in association with V-S offering, 'Lovely', is indeed a lovely blend of Punjabi folk ('Ni Main Yaar Manana Ni'-DAAG) with western and Arabic influences which appears to be a dance performance on stage with applauses in the background. Lyrics by Jiwan Mann and Kumaar are functional. The rap is good and the rendition(s) by Kanika Kapoor, Ravindra Upadhyay, Miraya Varma and Fateh even better. And one is sure that it would definitely be a treat to watch Deepika Padukone and SRK perform on screen on this eclectic dance number.

'Kamlee' (by Dr.Zeus, lyrics by Jiwan Mann) is another version of 'Lovely' and one must say that a lot of work has been done to give it a different touch, mood and feel.

'World Dance Medley' starts of as a medley of all the songs of the album and is extremely well crafted. However the highlight of the composition is definitely Shah Rukh Khan's superb oratory skill which stirs up feelings of patriotism.

Mika Singh goes absolutely off the rockers with 'Nonsense Ki Night', a hilarious song that does manage to bring a smile on the face, courtesy some sidesplitting and nonsensical lyrics by the director herself, Farah Khan and Vishal-Shekhar. The chorus 'I am feeling all right because it is a nonsense ki night' is cute and would definitely be patronized by kids. The feeling of freedom from stress and tension is conveyed well.

'Dance Like A Chammiya' is a cute item song; a dance number that is (once again) crafted like a stage dance track with whistles and applauses. The tune is surely dance worthy. Sunidhi Chauhan sings well and is supported by Vishal Dadlani.
