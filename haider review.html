Music Review : Haider
Director :  Vishal Bhardwaj
Music :  Vishal Bhardwaj
Lyrics :  Gulzar
Starring :  Shahid Kapoor, Shraddha Kapoor, Tabu, Irrfan Khan and Kay Kay Menon

September 17, 2014 12:56:43 PM IST

By Rafat, Glamsham Editorial

It is always a rejuvenating, gratifying and satiating experience listening to any musical offering from the house of Vishal Bhardwaj. The multi-talented and highly versatile director-composer-singer has especially excelled in the company of lyricist par excellence, living legend Gulzar Sahab; be it MAACHIS, KAMINEY, OMKARA, ISHQIYA etc. And one does look forward to their next musical offering, HAIDER, with humongous expectations, and hope for an encore of their previous brilliance. So let's check out with bated breath the HAIDER album!

Simply electrifying! One is simply bereft of words while expressing the feelings after listening to 'Aao Na'. Hard rock (bordering on acid) at its best. In fact it wouldn't be an exaggeration to say that this is one of the best (if not the best) original hard rock based number that one has heard so far in the history of Bollywood music. Hats off to both Vishals, composer Bhardwaj and Dadlani who sings his heart out here (almost rock legend Jim Morrison like), and of course Gulzar Sahab. Sample the lyrics- Na shaam, Na savera, Andhera hi andhera, Roohon ka basera, So Jao.

The second version 'So Jao' is more spine chilling as the rhythm is slackened on purpose, the meaning behind Gulzar Sahab's dark lyrics convey the theme of the film, based on Shakespeare's tragedy Hamlet. Sung by a host of singers- Bashir Lone, Bashir Bhawani, Muzammil Bhawani and Aalaap Majgavkar- this version is as superb as the first.

'Bismil' (Ghayal- Hindi; zakhmi-Urdu & wounded- English) is an enchanting Kashmiri folk based composition (complete with musical orchestrations like Santoor, Oud etc) rendered almost as a narrative by the eclectic Sukhwinder. The tune, especially in the 'mukhda', is very foot tapping. One has heard songs like 'Washmalle' (HINA) 'Bumbro' (MISSION KASHMIR), in the past which, have been more dance oriented but this one is like an exhilarating dance drama or 'nautanki'.

Another version of 'Bismil' is aptly titled 'Ek Aur Bismil' which makes for a good second hear with different instrumentations, more western and Arabic at its core.

Arijit Singh crooning for Vishal. A truly heavenly experience is how one can describe 'Khul Kabhi Toh', the passionate love song, truly Vishal Bhardwaj style, that features hot Shahid Kapoor and the beautiful Shraddha Kapoor.

Arijit Singh tries his hand at 'ghazal' singing and comes out with flying colours in 'Gulon Mein Rang Bhare' (Faiz Ahmad Faiz). However when compared to the original 'ghazal' legend, Mehdi Hasan's version, he falls short. But still it is a good experience hearing the evergreen popular ghazal.

The sound of aqua gurgling commences 'Jhelum' (the legendary Jhelum river of Kashmir) which is a sad, situational song about the lead desperately searching for his destination in times of despair. Vishal Bhardwaj's sweet and emotive vocals along with haunting melody and superb lyrics are the highlight of the situational.

The sad, separation number, 'Do Jahan' conveys the strong bond of love between the protagonists, and although the tune sounds a wee bit familiar, the song impresses because of the superb lyrics by the maestro. And also not to forget Suresh Wadkar's heart rending singing with good support by the lead actress Shraddha Kapoor (who sings in Kashmiri with aplomb).

Finally its Rekha Bhardwaj's turn to pay tribute to the suffering mothers, children, men, girls , widows of Kashmir who are paying a heavy price due to the militancy and insurgency (from across the border) via 'Aaj Ke Naam'. Once again Gulzar Sahab deserves a bow for his mastery.

Exhilarating! Haunting! Par excellence! This is just how one can sum up the musical offering from Vishal-Gulzar. Arguably their best after MAACHIS and OMKARA. All the songs are simply superb, classy (but has universal appeal, not restricted to just the class audience). Even the versions of the main songs are not mere space fillers. Our pick -the whole album! But the creme de la creme 'Aao Na' (both versions), 'Bismil' and 'Do Jahan'. The superb album will surely aid the cause of HAIDER when it releases all over on 2nd October. Slowly and surely the album will attain iconic status.
